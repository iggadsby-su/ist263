The Elements & Houses of Astrology

The 4 Elements

Fire

Aries, Leo, Sagittarius

Fire signs are passionate and bold, known for their high energy personalities. Natural charmers, these signs are charismatic, and good at taking over the energy of a room. Often associated with being the center of attention, fire signs tend to possess a magnetic social energy. However, these signs bold personalities can translate as aggressive, and their big personalities can come across as vein and egotistical.


Air

Gemini, Libra, Aquarius

Air signs are the element of intellect. Known for their strong social skills, these signs are often the life of the party, as they can talk about anything with anyone. They are great communicators and tend to think things through with logic instead of emotion. With that being said, as logic rules their communication, these signs can come off as cold or capricious, as they tend to act on what’s rational or fair, and not consider how their blunt thinking can hurt others feelings.


Water

Cancer, Scorpio, Pisces

Water signs are well known for their highly emotional nature, and strong intuition. Water is the element of emotions, meaning these signs are often highly in tune with their own emotions, whether they express them or not. This also tends to make them highly compassionate, with a strong ability to sense the emotions of others. Water signs are known for their dreamy nature and tend to be creative people. As they are ruled by their emotions, this can make these signs very sensitive and moody, allowing themselves to get easily hurt.


Earth

Taurus, Virgo, Capricorn

Earth signs are known for their grounding energy, often associated with a practical and loyal nature. They are patient and reliable, as they are not easily angered, and can always be counted on to deliver what they promise. On the other hand, these signs can be incredibly stubborn, and if it means they get to prove their point or be right, they love to let you know when you are wrong


The 12 Houses



Like a clock, the zodiac is divided into 12 houses, each ruling different aspects of your life. Within your birth chart, each sign falls into a specific house, depending on the time of your birth. When reading your chart, you blend the sign, house, and planet to better understand what that placement means specifically to you. Knowing what each house rules can help you dissect the meaning behind your chart. Below, I will explain the meaning of each house, and what they’re known for ruling.

House 1

The first house is concerned with all firsts and beginnings, it is the house of identity. Associated with first impressions, appearance, identity, new beginnings, and approach to life. The zodiac sign on the cusp of this house is what your rising or ascendent sign is… sound familiar?


House 2

The second house is associated with money, it is the house of income and covers all things in your material and physical environment. It is the house where we let desire take the wheel, and handles where we indulge ourself but also where we hold onto our money or what we find valuable. It is essentially the house of worth, and where we place that worth.


House 3

The third house is the house of communication. This house is concerned with things like talking, social media, siblings, friends, and our local environment. It influences our communication and how that plays out in these areas.


House 4

The fourth house is known as the house of home. It deals with the foundation of things and can be linked to family, ancestry, security, and privacy. This house is all about creating our private personal world, linked to the place the sun hides at midnight, in complete darkness or privacy.


House 5

The fifth house is the house of pleasure. Associated with all things that bring fulfillment, this house is concerned with romance, fun, children, sex, and creativity. It is all about joy and what brings you that joy.


House 6

The sixth house is known as the house of maintenance. It is concerned with conquering the daily tasks and challenges associated with life. More specifically this house is associated with schedules, fitness and diet, managing illness and injury, pets, coworkers, etc.. Truly the house of all things routine.


House 7

The seventh house is the house of relationships and other people in our life. This house concerns not only your friends, lovers, and business partners, but also your enemies. It deals with contracts and negations like getting married or signing a contract for a new business deal.


House 8

The eighth house of the zodiac is a mysterious house that is often misunderstood. It can be thought of as the house debt, and is concerned with birth and death, transformation, mysteries, and bonding at the deepest level. An example is the debt or baggage you bring with you into a relationship. It is more focused on other peoples stuff and how you deal with generosity and loss. It is where truths or opportunities that are hiding, rise to the surface.


House 9

The ninth house is the house of travel. This house is all about the journey, and not about quick trips. It is the house concerned with long-distance travel, higher education, philosophy, morals and ethics, and expansion. This house is about how the things you believe, in turn shape the world around you.


House 10

The tenth house is thought of as the house of career. It is concerned with all things career wise, like reputation, and public image. It governs things like fame, achievements, rules, and authority. The midheaven can be found in ones tenth house, which is the placement that represents your life purpose, and where you will shine in life.


House 11

The eleventh house is known as the house of friends. In this house is where you find everyone who supports you in your public life. It rules friendships, society, social justice, networking, and rebellion. This house is all about dreams and ambitions, as it is the place where your visions can be turned into a reality. Associated with the planet of Jupiter, which deals with expansion and luck, it is no surprise that this is the house that deals with making your dreams come true.


House 12

The twelfth and final house of the zodiac is the house of the unconscious, ruling all endings. It is a place where personal needs are sacrificed for the common good, teaching the lesson that not everything can be in your control. It covers the afterlife, old age, tying up loose-ends, and rules the imagination, dealing with creativity and the arts.
